Current Problems

The system is broken and hurting students

Financial Hardship

High fines disproportionately impact low-income students, turning minor mistakes into major financial burdens.

Unfair Appeals

Pay-to-play system denies due process to students who can't afford upfront payments.

Parking Scarcity

Limited spaces and first-year permit bans force students into impossible situations.

Hidden Programs

State-mandated payment assistance programs are buried and inaccessible.

Our Three-Point Reform Plan

Practical, proven solutions for a fairer system

1

First-Offense Warning Program

Replace financial penalties for first-time minor violations with educational warnings. Students can complete the "Gaucho Parking Primer" quiz to have their first ticket forgiven, transforming punishment into learning.

Education over punishment

Transform your first mistake into a learning opportunity

Access payment plans when you need them most

2

Accessible Payment Plans

Overhaul the payment plan program to comply with California law. Create an online portal, extend application windows to 120 days, and proactively inform students of available financial assistance.

Legal compliance with CA Vehicle Code 40220
3

Fair Appeals Process

Eliminate the requirement to pre-pay fines before contesting them. Establish a student-majority Parking Appeals Committee to ensure peer review and restore true due process.

Student-majority review committee

Justice shouldn't depend on your bank account
